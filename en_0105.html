Air and particles are inhaled through the nose and mouth. They then travel down the respiratory tract (pharynx, larynx and trachea) into the lungs via two bronchial tubes/bronchi, which lead into smaller tubes called bronchioles that end with tiny air sacs called alveoli.

Particles are deposited along the respiratory tract depending on their size. Larger particles are removed in the nasal passages and smaller particles can penetrate as far as the alveoli.

Very small particles can be particularly hazardous to human health because the alveoli membrane, where the oxygen (O2) and carbon dioxide (CO2) gas exchange takes place, is extremely thin.
