Fire Marshal Directives, Base/Wing/Formation Standing Orders, and Unit Standing Operating Procedures stem from the RPP and offer more detailed instructions on how to carry out the DND/CAF RPP using local resources.

Select each for details.

Fire Marshal Directives (FMDs)

FMDs are designed to explain the procedures to follow for aspects of the RPP that require more specific departmental guidance than is provided elsewhere. There are currently eight FMDs for the RPP. Two of them are intended strictly for Fire Services Personnel (bolded), while the other six generally apply to DND/CAF.

The following FMDs can be found under ADM(IE)'s approved policies in SharePoint.

  • FMD 1001 Firefighter Protective Ensembles
  • FMD 1003 Quantitative Fit Testing (QNFT) of Respirators Using the Particle Counting Instrument Protocol
  • FMD 1006 Respiratory Protection Program (RPP) Records Management Requirements
  • FMD 1010 Respiratory Protection Program Administrator Certification
  • FMD 1040 Respiratory Protection Program Air Quality Management
  • FMD 2004 Wildland Fire Operations
  • FMD 3003 SCBA: Operation, Inspection and Maintenance
  • FMD 3006 Selection, Installation, Operation, and Maintenance of High Pressure Compressed Breathing Air Systems (CBAS)

Standing Orders

A standing order specifies the roles and responsibilities from Part 1 of the DND RPP for all local (Base/Wing/Formation) RPP stakeholders.

At a minimum, the standing orders must contain information on the following roles: user, manager, supervisor, fit tester, RPPA, Preventive Medicine Technician and Health Canada's Public Service Occupational Health Program (PSOHP) General Safety Officer/Advisor and the CFFM

In other words, a standing order must specify who does fit testing, who does air quality testing, who does annual walkaround inspections, who is responsible for ensuring that OHWAs and occupational health evaluations are completed, who ensures training and fit testing is completed and tracked, etc.

Unit Standing Operating Procedures (SOPs)

Unit Safety SOPs and/or Unit RPP SOPs (also called Standard Operating Procedures) are detailed instructions pertaining to a specific unit or physical location. SOPs are written by the supervisor or RPPA and must adhere to legislation, regulations, standards, directives and orders, and to manufacturer instructions and recommendations.

SOPs are posted in the workplace as a hard or soft copy.
