Three L1s collaborate on the structure and basic application of the RPP. These L1s are ADM(IE), CMP/MILPERSCOM and ADM(Mat).

Users

RPD users are responsible for:

  • monitoring their own health and safety and that of others in the same area
  • reporting to their supervisor or other responsible person any condition or change that can impact their ability to use an RPD safely
  • learning how to use an RPD and its components properly and as needed in accordance with training received and with employer and manufacturer instructions
  • taking care of their equipment including proper cleaning, disinfecting, storing, inspecting for deficiencies before and after use, recording any deficiencies found, and submitting RPDs and/or their components for repair or disposal as required
  • ensuring that they remain clean-shaven and that they don't have any object or material interfering with the facepiece seal of a tight-fitting RPD; users must be in the same condition as when they presented themselves for fit testing

Managers of RPD Users

Managers of RPD Users are responsible for:

  • ensuring that pre-placement, periodic and fitness to work evaluations (FTWEs) occupational health evaluations are completed as required

Details on manager responsibilities are available in the Respiratory Protection Program for Managers and Supervisors course.

Supervisors of RPD Users

Supervisors of RPD Users

Supervisors of RPD users are responsible for:

  • ensuring that RPD users receive the RPDs and equipment they need to do their work safely
  • ensuring that RPD users receive training on the safe use of RPDs and that they use their RPDs according to the training they received

    • Details on supervisor responsibilities are available in the Respiratory Protection Program for Managers and Supervisors course.

Base/Wing Commanders and Unit Commanding Officers (COs)

Base/Wing Commanders and Unit Commanding Officers (COs)

Base/Wing Commanders and Unit COs are responsible for:

  • developing and implementing an RPP for their area of responsibility, and ensuring the overall success of that RPP
  • appointing an RPPA
  • managing the budget to ensure that RPP requirements are met and maintained on the base
  • ensuring that the unit purchases approved RPDs

Respiratory Protection Program Administrators (RPPAs)

Respiratory Protection Program Administrators (RPPAs)

Local RPPAs are responsible for:

  • monitoring the proper use of RPDs
  • overseeing fit testers and managing the local Fit Testing Program
  • appointing and managing Respiratory Protection Program Coordinators (RPPCs) and delegating RPP tasks to them
  • overseeing air quality testing (usually delegated to an RPPC)
  • ensuring that new breathing air compressors are installed correctly and that any contracts for installation follow the CSA requirements
  • advising on local RPP matters
  • advising the CO on how to provide an effective RPP
Note

Some RPPAs are also fit testers, but not all fit testers are RPPAs. Most RPPAs are Fire Chiefs or Chief Fire Inspectors, and most fit testers are firefighters or RPPCs.

Fire Chiefs have so many duties in addition to managing staff at a fire hall that they don't typically do fit testing or air quality testing.

Details on RPPA responsibilities are available in the Respiratory Protection Program for Administrators course.

Fit Testers

Fit testers are responsible for

  • confirming with the manager/supervisor that the following requirements are met prior to fit testing a user:
    • the user is up to date on pre-placement/periodic health evaluations
    • the Occupational Health Workplace Assessment (OHWA) Report identifying the RPD(s) to be fit tested is up to date for the hazards of the work task(s)
    • the appropriate fit testing form has been filled out
  • helping RPD users select a suitable RPD (comfort, sizing) from the list available for their assigned work
  • obtaining an assortment of types and sizes of commonly fit tested respirator brands
  • ensuring that RPD users observe appropriate written standing operating procedures (SOPs) during the fit test
  • interpreting fit testing results
  • creating, maintaining and retaining records for the maintenance, calibration and repair of fit testing equipment

Details on fit tester responsibilities are available in the Respiratory Protection Program for Fit Testers course.

Canadian Forces Fire Marshal (CFFM)

The Canadian Forces Fire Marshal (CFFM) and their Office on behalf of the Assistant Deputy Minister (Infrastructure and Environment) (ADM(IE)) are responsible for:

  • developing, coordinating, promoting, and administering the national RPP and policy according to the DND RPP (a collaborative effort with DFHP), Fire Marshal Directives (FMDs), and in consultation with in-house and legislative documents
  • managing the National RPPA (NRPPA) who leads the local RPPAs across DND/CAF
  • providing RPP training to RPPAs, fit testers, RPD users, managers, and supervisors, in compliance with the DND RPP
  • providing guidance on formulating and implementing RPPs to NDHQ groups, commands, bases, wings, and units
  • managing, administering, and funding contracts for air quality testing of RPP equipment (breathing air from fixed or portable compressors) at DND/CAF bases, wings, and units in Canada
  • conducting compliance reviews of the RPP every three years

Director Forces Health Protection (DFHP)

The Director Forces Health Protection (DFHP) is responsible for:

  • developing and promoting the national RPP and policy according to the DND RPP (a collaborative effort with CFFM), Health Protection Recommendations and other in-house guidance/reference documents
  • guiding the Preventive Medicine Technicians/Environmental Health Officers in their work
  • other functions as outlined in DAOD 5021-1 (under DGHS) and the DND RPP, Part I, Program and Responsibilities

Other Roles

Other RPP stakeholders and their roles include:

  • Assistant Deputy Minister (Materiel) (ADM(Mat)) provides advice on procuring high financial value equipment such as fixed breathing air compressors.
  • Deputy Chief of Staff Medical Policy (DCOS D Med Pol) provides information on pre-placement and periodic health evaluations for military personnel.
  • Preventive Medicine Technicians, Environmental Health Officers, and Occupational Hygienists are responsible for:
    • advice on respirator selection
    • assessments of RPDs and equipment, of engineering and administrative controls, and of hazards that would necessitate such controls.
  • Health Canada conducts health evaluations for public service employees under the Public Service Occupational Health Program.
  • General Practitioners (family doctors) conduct health evaluations when Health Canada or other designated doctors are unavailable (for public service employees only).
  • Air quality testing service providers provide the air quality test kits which are used to test breathing air quality from breathing air compressors.
  • Directorate of General Safety (D Safe G) provides the framework for many safety principles such as the hierarchy of controls, principles for deviations from safety policies, work refusals, etc.
